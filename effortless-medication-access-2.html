What are GLP-1 medications and how do they work?

Drawing upon the capabilities of continuous glucose monitoring (CGM) and other essential health parameters, our board-certified clinicians provide medication management customized to each patient's specific health needs.

Our clinicians prescribe FDA-approved medications designed for Type 2 diabetes management and weight control. We don’t follow a "one-size-fits-all" approach, instead we rely on an individual's unique health indicators to determine the safest and most effective medication(s) and dosage of medications, if necessary.
Through our partnering pharmacy, medications can be conveniently delivered directly to your doorstep, eliminating the inconvenience of waiting in long lines at your local pharmacy. Our dedicated pharmacy specialists will also collaborate with your insurance provider to ensure your medication coverage.
Our expert clinicians can prescribe from several available medications based on your medical needs
Some of the medications that can be prescribed are approved for both Type 2 Diabetes as well as weight management. These medications include a special class called GLP-1 agonists (Glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonists).
You may have heard of GLP-1 medications like Ozempic or Wegovy in the news. And for good reason, these medications have been shown to be clinically effective in Type 2 Diabetes and weight management.

In Type 2 Diabetes, these medications work by activating GLP-1 receptors in the pancreas, which leads to enhanced insulin release and reduced glucagon release (the hormone that stimulates glucose production when your blood sugar is too low).

For weight management, these medications also act on the gut, slowing gastric emptying and making you feel full for longer. They also act on areas of the brain responsible for regulating appetite and calorie intake.

What if a GLP-1 medication doesn’t work for me?
GLP-1 medications are not indicated for all patients. However, there are a variety of medications used in Type 2 Diabetes and weight management. Our expert clinicians will find the right medications for you.
Some of the GLP-1 medications include
Ozempic and Wegovy (Semaglutide)
Saxenda and Victoza (Liraglutide)
Mounjaro (Tirzepatide)
